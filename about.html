Field Mechanical Engineering

My research focuses on designing robotic mechanisms that can adapt their geometry and configuration according to their environment and task.

I am currently developing a modular continuum robot composed of independently controllable rigid segments. Each segment incorporates an SMA-actuated mechanical locking mechanism, allowing the robot to selectively unlock, move, and maintain its posture without continuous power consumption.

This architecture enables the robot to form diverse configurations, observe a target from multiple directions, avoid obstacles, and expand its accessible workspace in confined environments.

Alongside mechanism design, I study vision-based workspace measurement, SMA actuator performance, thermal behavior, repeatability, and energy consumption.
